Our vision

Besides fostering collaborations, our vision is for 3 or 4 subgroups focused on neuro-anatomy, imaging, image processing, and applications to evolve organically out of this symposium and subsequent meetings. We also hope to put out position papers, share standardized data sets with manual labels, and disseminate new cutting edge methods for thalamic segmentation, and meet in person, of course, in the near future !

This group has identified prevailing and future challenges and has shaped-up recommended future directions which include:

Cytoarchitecture of the thalamus

An appraisal of the necessity for an agreement between knowledge of the cytoarchitecture of the human thalamus and how reproducible it has been across neuroimaging studies.

MRI acquisition sequences

A brief review and analysis of current and upcoming MRI acquisition sequences for the human thalamus and its nuclei. The ability to use other MR contrasts than standard anatomical scans, like those sensitised for water diffusion, myelin- and iron-content, or blood-oxygen-level-dependent (BOLD) signal, to capture differences in microstructural composition and/or connectivity across thalamic nuclei that could provide complementary information to segmentation algorithms, will be discussed.

Thalamic nuclei segmentation

An accurate comparison of existing and promising thalamic nuclei segmentation methods, their strengths and weaknesses and how they can/should be further refined. The level of detail achievable using 7T MRI gains substantially, providing features that remained undetectable in-vivo before. In that respect, the ability of artificially intelligent methods to "dig" into the data beyond the traditional physical properties in an image is worth (a) promoting and (b) used with an aim to harmonize methods so that the congruence will at least be assured from this point of view.

Common
framework

Propose of a framework for working together towards harmonizing imaging data on the human thalamus and its nuclei in terms of image acquisition, processing, and quantification. Through this, the interdisciplinary community working on the thalamus, will be able to set gold standards that will then allow us to rapidly progress our understanding of the role of the thalamus in health and disease.
